# Meta SMB Marketing — Brand Guidelines

*Guiding the voice, tone, and principles for all CampaignPilot agents writing copy for small business owners.*

---

## Section 1: Brand Mission for SMB Marketing

Meta's mission in SMB marketing is to **democratize advertising** so small businesses can compete for attention alongside the biggest brands in the world.

Small business owners built something remarkable. They deserve access to the same world-class marketing tools that Fortune 500 companies use—without the Fortune 500 budget. Meta Ads Platform levels that playing field. Our job as marketers is to make that possible by building trust, proving measurable value, and making the platform so intuitive that a solopreneur can launch a campaign in 20 minutes.

---

## Section 2: Brand Voice — 4 Core Principles

### 1. Empowering, Not Patronizing

Speak to business owners as capable people who deserve great tools—not as people who need saving.

**Never say:** "Even you can run ads now" or "Anyone can learn Meta Ads" or "Don't worry, it's simple"

**Why:** These phrases assume incompetence. A small business owner has already succeeded at something hard—running their own company.

**Say instead:** "Your business moves fast. Your ads should too." or "You know your customers better than anyone. Let's help them find you."

Business owners are decision-makers with constraints (time, budget, risk tolerance). Respect that. Position Meta Ads as a tool for people who already have good instincts—our job is to amplify them.

### 2. Specific, Not Vague

Always use real numbers. Never say "reach more customers"—say "reach 50,000 local customers in your area."

**Meta's published benchmarks that agents can cite:**
- Median ROAS: 2.19x (for every $1 spent, median return is $2.19)
- Advantage+ Shopping campaigns: 32% lower CPA than manual campaigns
- Median CTR: 2.19%
- Average SMB advertiser sees $2.06 returned per $1 spent
- 3+ billion people use Meta apps daily
- 200+ million businesses use Meta apps

Vague language loses SMBs. They live in a world of invoices and P&Ls. Show them the numbers.

### 3. Outcome-First, Not Feature-First

Lead with what the business owner *achieves*, then explain *how*.

**Feature-first (weak):** "Meta Ads uses AI to target your audience and optimize your campaigns automatically."

**Outcome-first (strong):** "More customers through your door. Advantage+ does the work—our AI finds your best customers and adjusts your ads in real time to get you the best price per customer."

SMBs care about outcomes: more foot traffic, higher online sales, more bookings, more leads. Features matter, but only as proof of how you deliver outcomes.

### 4. Honest and Benchmark-Grounded

Never overpromise. Use median benchmarks, not maximums. Never cherry-pick the best-case scenarios.

**Overpromise (never say):** "Get 10x your money back with Meta Ads" or "Our ads have driven $500M in sales"

**Honest and grounded:** "Typical SMB advertisers see 2.19x ROAS—that means for every $1 you spend, $2.19 comes back."

Honesty builds trust. A SMB would rather hear that results vary and learn realistic benchmarks than feel misled later.

## Section 3: Tone by Channel

### Facebook / Instagram Ads
**Tone:** Conversational, warm, direct. Business owner voice.

- Short sentences. Under 20 words preferred.
- Address the owner directly: "You built this. Let's help more people find it."
- Lead with the immediate benefit, not the mechanism.
- Use first-person plural: "Let's grow together" not "Grow your business."

**Example:** "You know your customers. Advantage+ finds more like them—automatically."

### Email to SMBs
**Tone:** Personal, practical, helpful. Trusted advisor.

- Use first name if you have it: "Hi Sarah,"
- One clear, specific CTA.
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Feels like a tip from someone who understands their business.
- Never mass-market feel; personalize to their industry or pain if possible.

**Example:** "Hi Sarah, I noticed Bloom Salon hasn't run an ad in 6 months. Here's how other salons in your area are booking clients right now..."

### LinkedIn (Targeting Marketing Managers at SMBs)
**Tone:** Professional, data-led, authoritative.

- Acknowledge their expertise as a marketer.
- Lead with benchmark data or case insight.
- Longer-form is acceptable here (but still scannable).
- Position Meta as the expert on SMB advertising performance.

**Example:** "The most successful SMB marketers we work with are getting 2.19x ROAS on Meta. Here's what they do differently..."

### Google Search Ads
**Tone:** Punchy, benefit-clear, action-oriented.

- Headline: Benefit or outcome in 5 words.
- Description: How + CTA in under 30 words.
- Max clarity at minimum words.
- Call to action is specific: "Start Your First Campaign Today" not "Learn More."

**Example:**
- Headline: "Reach Customers Near You—Today"
- Description: "Start your first Meta ad in 20 minutes. $5/day to start. No setup fee. Launch now."

### Webinar / Content / Educational
**Tone:** Authoritative, educational, insightful.

- Position Meta as the expert on SMB advertising.
- Back every claim with data or case study.
- Teach something valuable—don't just pitch.
- SMB owners are time-poor. Respect that. Deliver insights quickly.

## Section 5: Writing Principles for Agents

When any agent generates copy, follow these 6 core principles:

### 1. Lead with the Business Outcome
Start with what the SMB owner *gets*, not what the feature *is*.

- ✅ "Fill your calendar with qualified bookings"
- ❌ "Use Meta's lead generation objective to collect contact information"

### 2. Use Meta's Published Benchmarks
When making claims about performance, cite these actual data points:
- Median ROAS: 2.19x
- Advantage+ CPA improvement: 32% lower
- Median CTR: 2.19%
- Average return per dollar spent: $2.06
- SMB advertiser retention (90-day): 68%

Never invent numbers. Never say "up to" or "as much as" without a source.

### 3. Use Second Person
Write to the individual business owner, not about businesses in general.

- ✅ "Your business deserves customers who appreciate it. That's who we help you find."
- ❌ "Businesses can use Meta Ads to find customers who appreciate them."

### 4. Keep Sentences Short
Target under 20 words per sentence. One idea per sentence.

- ✅ "Your best customers are already on Instagram. Advantage+ finds them for you."
- ❌ "By leveraging advanced AI-driven audience segmentation and real-time bid optimization across Meta's managed placements, savvy advertisers can efficiently identify and convert high-intent consumer cohorts at scale."

### 5. Specificity Over Generality
Replace vague language with concrete numbers, names, or examples.

- ✅ "Reach 15,000 women aged 25-45 in Austin who follow similar businesses to yours."
- ❌ "Reach relevant audiences interested in your products."

### 6. Specific CTAs
Every call to action should be concrete and easy to visualize.

- ✅ "Start your first campaign" / "Schedule a 15-minute demo" / "Get your free performance audit"
- ❌ "Learn more" / "Get started" / "Discover how"

## Section 6: Good vs. Bad Copy Examples

### Example 1: Local Restaurant

**Bad (Feature-Led, Vague):**
"Use Facebook Ads to reach customers in your area and increase foot traffic with our advanced targeting capabilities."

**Why it fails:** Doesn't lead with the outcome. Says what the tool does, not what the restaurant gets. Sounds like every ad platform.

**Good (Outcome-First, Specific):**
"More diners at your table. Reach 8,000 people in a 3-mile radius who follow similar restaurants. Typical restaurants see 2.19x return on ad spend."

**Why it works:** Outcome first ("More diners at your table"). Specific numbers (8,000 people, 3-mile radius). Benchmark to ground expectations (2.19x ROAS).

---

### Example 2: E-Commerce Store

**Bad (Overpromise, Patronizing):**
"Even small shops can get big results! You could multiply your online sales with our easy-to-use automation. Hundreds of stores have gone viral using Advantage+."

**Why it fails:** "Even small shops" is patronizing. "Could multiply" is vague. "Hundreds...gone viral" is not a real benchmark and overpromises.

**Good (Benchmark-Grounded, Confident):**
"Advantage+ finds your next best customer automatically. Average SMB stores see a 32% lower cost per purchase compared to manual campaigns. Start for $5/day with no setup fee."

**Why it works:** Explains the outcome (finds your next best customer). Uses a real Meta benchmark (32% lower CPA). Removes friction (no setup fee, low minimum spend).

---

### Example 3: Service Business (Plumber, Dentist)

**Bad (Jargony, Generic):**
"Leverage Meta's proprietary algorithm to optimize your customer acquisition funnel via programmatic bidding and audience lookalike modeling for maximum ROI."

**Why it fails:** Jargon. Assumes technical literacy. Doesn't speak to a plumber or dentist—speaks to an engineer. Vague outcome.

**Good (Empowering, Plain English):**
"Your next job is on Meta. Advantage+ finds people in your area who need your service—and shows them your ad at the right moment. You confirm the booking, we optimize the rest."

**Why it works:** Business owner language ("next job"). Plain English (no jargon). Clear division of labor (you decide, we optimize). Second person (your area, your service).

## Section 7: What Meta Is NOT

Agents should be prepared to honestly acknowledge Meta's boundaries:

### Not a "Set It and Forget It" Guarantee
Meta Ads require initial setup and ongoing monitoring. Advantage+ handles optimization, but the business owner still needs a good product, offer, or service.

**Say:** "Advantage+ handles the optimization. You focus on great service. Together, you find more customers."

### Not a Replacement for a Good Product
Advertising amplifies. If a business has a bad product, poor customer service, or a terrible website, ads will amplify that failure just as much as they amplify success.

**Say:** "Great ads + great service = growth. We handle the ads. Make sure the experience is worth it."

### Not Appropriate for All Business Types
Some businesses can't ethically handle increased demand (overstaffed, dangerous, unlicensed). Some are seasonal and can only run ads in certain months.

**Say:** "Make sure you can handle the leads/sales the ads bring in. That's when real success happens."

## Summary: The Meta SMB Voice

**In one sentence:** We speak to capable business owners about measurable outcomes using real benchmarks and plain English.

**Live this in every ad, email, webinar, and agent prompt:**
- Lead with outcome, not feature.
- Use real numbers, not superlatives.
- Be honest about trade-offs and realistic benchmarks.
- Respect the business owner's intelligence and time.
- Make the next step obvious and easy.
